Phases of France's Transition

  • 1789-1794 (The World Turned Upside Down)

    • An elected legislature turns France into a republic and imposes its egalitarian ideology onto the nation. Feudal dues are abolished. Aristocratic and Church lands are sold to speculators.

  • 1794-1799 (Instability)

    • The French Republic is increasingly dependent on playing Left and Right against each other. This balancing does not prevent multiple coup d'etat.

  • 1799-1815 (Napoleon)

    • Napoleon establishes a dictatorship that evolves into a constitutional monarchy.

  • 1815-1830 (Restoration)

    • The restored House of Bourbon hedges on the question of constitutionalism.

  • 1830-1848 (July Monarchy)

    • France becomes a constitutional monarchy under the House of Orleans following the July Revolution.

  • 1848-1851 (Second Republic)

    • Disappointment with the constitutional monarchy of the House of Orleans leads to another revolution, establishing the Second French Republic.

  • 1852-1870 (Second Empire)

    • The Republic is overthrown when its President, the third Napoleon, reestablishes the empire of his uncle, the first Napoleon.

  • 1870 (Third Republic Formed)

    • In 1870, after the capture of Napoleon III by the Prussian Army, the legislature declares the monarchy defunct, establishing France's Third Republic.

Revolutions Podcast

From the French Revolution of 1789 to the Third French Republic established in 1870, French politics was a series of revolutions and counter-revolutionary restorations, featuring three main factions struggling for power. This page will serve the reader as a guide to these factions.

France, at the time, was divided between the Left and the Right. The Right itself was divided between supporters of three rival dynasties while the Left advocated a democratic republic. The Left was militantly secular and at times persecuted people who adhered to the Catholic faith of the majority of the French. The Right sought to maintain government-enforced Catholicism though to what degree depended on what dynasty was given allegiance and which monarch led the dynasty at any given time.
